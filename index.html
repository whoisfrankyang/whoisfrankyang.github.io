Hey! My name is Bohan Yang, an undergradute student at Cornell University, majoring in Computer Science and minor in Artificial Intelligence and Business.

My passion lies at the intersection of robot learning and deep learning. I am particularly interested in how we can use AI to make the world a better place whether through research or through entrepreneurship.

Currently, I am a robotics researcher at the Cornell EmPRISE Lab, advised by Prof. Tapomayukh Bhattacharjee. My recent work includes developing an Adaptive Robot Query System for Task Failure Recovery, which enables robots to proactively ask for human help when uncertain, and autonomously learn recovery strategies from failed tasks.

Before that, I was a research assistant at the Cornell MAE Department advised by Prof. Nikolaos Bouklas, working on LLM-Guided Differential Equation Inference via Symbolic Regression; and before that, I was a research assistant advised by Prof. Weiqing Gu from Harvey Mudd College working on Concussion Diagnosis using EEG and machine learning.

Outside of academia, I interned at Dasion as a machine learning software engineer during the summers of 2023 and 2024, and continued part-time during the academic year. I contributed to the development of intelligent voice agents for elder care and helped improve the performance and reliability of natural language understanding systems used in real-world applications.

In summer of 2024, I co-founded Pixelate Inc., a startup focused on developing AI-powered CAD Copilot tools for engineers, designers, and makers. Please check out our website to learn more about our work!

I am always eager to collaborate with fellow researchers and industry professionals, and I am committed to continuous learning and growth in these dynamic fields. Let's connect and explore the possibilities together!

Projects

DXF2CAD

Developing a generative AI framework that learns from 2D DXF blueprints to construct accurate, editable 3D CAD models in STEP format in seconds.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, Transformers

CADParser

Reimplemented a transformer-based model that learns to generate editable construction sequences from 3D B-Rep CAD geometry, enabling parametric design automation and model reconstruction.

Technologies: Python, PyTorch, Transformers, Graph Neural Networks (DGL)

MPI in Diffusion Modeling

A high-performance parallel simulation of 2D diffusion using MPI. system scales across processors, enabling efficient modeling of large, high-resolution grids for scientific computation.

Technologies: C++, MPI, High Performance Computing

Address Matching

Developed methods to detect and reduce inconsistencies in healthcare provider directories to improve data reliability and patient access. Leveraged multi-source data comparison and explored scoring techniques to validate provider locations.

Technologies: Python, Pandas, Locality Sensitive Hashing (LSH)
