Hey! My name is Bohan Yang, an undergradute student at Cornell University, majoring in Computer Science and minor in Artificial Intelligence and Robotics.

My passion lies at the intersection of robot learning and deep learning. I am particularly interested in how autonomous systems can learn to understand, predict, and respond to the real world with minimal human intervention.

Currently, I am a robotics researcher at the Cornell EmPRISE Lab (link), advised by Prof. Tapo Bhattacharjee. My recent work includes developing an Adaptive Robot Query System for Task Failure Recovery, which enables robots to proactively ask for human help when uncertain, and autonomously learn recovery strategies from failed tasks.

Before that, I was a research assistant at the Cornell MAE Department advised by Prof. Nikolaos Bouklas, working on LLM-Guided Differential Equation Inference via Symbolic Regression; and before that, I was a research assistant advised by Prof. Weiqing Gu from Harvey Mudd College wokring on Concussion Diagnosis using EEG and machine learning.

In parallel, I co-founded Pixelate, a startup focused on developing AI-powered CAD Copilot tools for engineers, designers, and makers. You can visit our website for more information.

I am always eager to collaborate with fellow researchers and industry professionals, and I am committed to continuous learning and growth in these dynamic fields. Let's connect and explore the possibilities together!
